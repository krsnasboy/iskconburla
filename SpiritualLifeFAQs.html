Spiritual Life FAQs

Find answers to common questions about spiritual practices, Krishna consciousness, and temple activities.

What is Krishna consciousness?

Krishna consciousness is the awakening of our natural state of being as eternal servants of Lord Krishna. It's a process of reconnecting with the Supreme Personality of Godhead through devotional service (bhakti-yoga), which includes chanting, hearing about Krishna, and engaging in loving activities for His pleasure.

Why do we chant Hare Krishna?

The Hare Krishna maha-mantra is a transcendental sound vibration that directly connects us with Krishna. Chanting cleanses the heart of material desires and awakens our dormant love for God. The mantra consists of three names of the Supreme: Hare, Krishna, and Rama, which are all spiritual energies of the Lord.

What is the soul according to Vedic philosophy?

The soul (atma) is an eternal, individual particle of spirit, distinct from the temporary material body. It is described in Bhagavad-gita as immutable, indestructible, and full of consciousness. The soul's natural position is to serve Krishna with love and devotion.

What is the significance of temple worship?

Temple worship allows us to directly serve the deity forms of the Lord with love and devotion. The deities are not idols but merciful manifestations of Krishna that accept our service. By engaging in temple activities, we purify our consciousness and develop our relationship with God.

Why do devotees follow a vegetarian diet?

Vegetarianism aligns with the principle of ahimsa (non-violence) and recognizes that all living beings are souls. Food offered to Krishna with love (prasadam) becomes spiritualized and helps purify our consciousness. Meat eating involves violence and keeps us bound to material consciousness.

What are the nine processes of devotional service?

The nine processes are: hearing (sravanam), chanting (kirtanam), remembering (visnoh smaranam), serving the lotus feet (pada-sevanam), worshiping (arcanam), offering prayers (vandanam), carrying out orders (dasyam), being a friend (sakhyam), and surrendering everything (atma-nivedanam). These purify the heart and awaken love for Krishna.

What is karma and how does it affect us?

Karma refers to the universal law of cause and effect governing material existence. Every action has a reaction that binds us to the cycle of birth and death. Bhakti-yoga transcends karma by connecting us directly with Krishna, who can nullify all karmic reactions when we surrender to Him.

What is the daily schedule at ISKCON temples?

A typical schedule includes Mangala Arati (early morning worship), Tulasi puja, guru-puja, Srimad-Bhagavatam class, noon arati, evening arati, and Bhagavad-gita class. There's also time for personal japa (chanting), deity service, and preaching activities. Schedules may vary slightly by temple.

How many rounds of japa should I chant daily?

Initiated devotees commit to chanting 16 rounds daily (about 2 hours). Beginners can start with whatever number is comfortable (even 1-2 rounds) and gradually increase. The key is regularity and quality - chanting with attention to the sound and meaning of each mantra.

What is the role of a spiritual master?

The bona fide spiritual master is Krishna's representative who guides disciples back to Godhead. He teaches by example and gives proper knowledge of the scriptures. By serving and following the guru's instructions with faith, one makes rapid spiritual progress and develops love for Krishna.

What is the difference between soul and Supersoul?

The individual soul (atma) is a tiny spiritual particle that animates one body, while the Supersoul (Paramatma) is Krishna's expansion that resides in all beings as the witness and permitter. The Supersoul guides the soul according to its desires, and both are distinct yet connected aspects of the Absolute Truth.

Can anyone visit ISKCON temples?

Yes, ISKCON temples welcome everyone regardless of background or belief. Visitors should dress modestly (covered shoulders and knees) and observe temple etiquette like removing shoes before entering deity rooms. All are invited to participate in kirtan, classes, and feast offerings.

Why do devotees wear tilak?

Tilak (clay markings) identifies the body as a temple of Vishnu and reminds us of our spiritual identity. The twelve parts represent Krishna's lotus feet, with the center marking indicating His worship in the heart. It's both a purification ritual and an external sign of devotion.

What is the importance of Srimad-Bhagavatam?

Srimad-Bhagavatam is the mature fruit of Vedic knowledge, exclusively describing Krishna's names, forms, qualities, and pastimes. Studying it nourishes the soul with transcendental knowledge and devotion. Srila Prabhupada called it the "spotless Purana" that delivers one from material existence.

What happens after death according to Vedic wisdom?

The soul leaves the current body and enters a new one according to its karma and desires. Devotees of Krishna transcend this cycle and return to the spiritual world. The Bhagavad-gita explains that whatever state of being one remembers at death, that state they will attain in the next life.

What is prasadam and why is it important?

Prasadam means "mercy" - food that's first offered to Krishna with love and devotion. Eating prasadam purifies our consciousness, destroys sinful reactions, and helps us remember Krishna. The Sunday Feast is ISKCON's signature prasadam distribution program that attracts thousands worldwide.

How can I balance spiritual life with worldly duties?

Krishna consciousness isn't about abandoning responsibilities but spiritualizing them. Offer your work to Krishna, maintain regular sadhana (spiritual practices), associate with devotees, and gradually spiritualize all activities. Even household life can be yoga when centered around serving Krishna.

What is the significance of Ekadashi?

Ekadashi (the 11th day after each new and full moon) is especially auspicious for fasting and increased spiritual practices. By observing Ekadashi properly (abstaining from grains and beans), one gains special spiritual merit and purification. It's a day to focus more on hearing, chanting, and remembering Krishna.

What is maya in Krishna consciousness?

Maya is Krishna's external energy that creates the illusion that we're separate from Him and can enjoy independently. It covers our true spiritual nature and binds us to temporary material existence. By taking shelter of Krishna through bhakti, we can overcome maya's influence.

How can I deepen my Krishna consciousness?

Key practices include: 1) Regular chanting of Hare Krishna, 2) Daily scripture study, 3) Associating with devotees, 4) Serving the mission, 5) Following the four regulative principles, 6) Hearing from senior devotees, and 7) Cultivating gratitude. Progress comes through consistent practice and surrender to Krishna.
